 About the Climate and Health report

As the climate changes, we track New York City's risk for extreme heat, storms and flooding, and episodes of elevated ozone. These have important public health impacts: already, heat kills more people than all other natural disasters.

  • Environmental factors that can help protect people - or help us understand a neighborhood's risk
  • Special populations that are more vulnerable to storms, power outages, and heat events
  • Health outcomes like heat-related illness

Indicator ranking

Each indicator is placed in one of three categories:


This shows us how the neighborhood compares to other NYC neighborhoods, if you ranked all of the NYC neighborhoods based on this indicator.

  • Better: in the top 1/3rd of all NYC neighorhoods.
  • Middle means that it's close to the NYC median - ranking somewhere in the middle third of all neighborhoods.
  • Worse: in the bottom 1/3rd of NYC neighborhoods.

These are only based on the distribution of data values, not the range. The absolute values of indicators in different categoeis may not be all that different.

Each indicator button includes the rank: how that neighborhood scores out of all 42 NYC neighborhoods, based on that indicator.


To see more data on an indicator and to understand how it compares, click the indicator button.
